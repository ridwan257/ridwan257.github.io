The Journey So Far

Who Am I?

My academic journey began with a Bachelor's degree in Genetic Engineering and Biotechnology in 2019, though my childhood dream was to study Physics. During my undergraduate years, I faced personal struggles and wasn't enjoying my departmental studies. This led to feelings of frustration and depression, which impacted my academic performance. In the covid pandemic, I found myself at a crossroads, unsure of what to do next. I was just following my mind what it says. I was just doing some online courses on mathematics and following some youtube channels. I felt frustrated and lost at times but these self-driven efforts gave me new direction. The more I delved into these topics, the more I realized how powerful computational methods could be in understanding biological systems.

This realization sparked my passion for computational biology. The frustration and confusion I had experienced earlier gave way to a renewed motivation, which completely changed the trajectory of my academic path. I became determined to pursue a career where I could merge my interests in biology and computational methods to tackle complex problems. Looking ahead, my dream is to pursue a Ph.D. in Computational Biology, where I can continue to explore the integration of computational methods with biological research. I am particularly interested in using advanced mathematical and computational models to address challenges in genomics, disease research, and personalized medicine.
