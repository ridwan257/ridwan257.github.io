Who Am I?

I’m a computational biology enthusiast with a background in Genetic Engineering and Biotechnology, though my early interests was to study physics. During my undergraduate years, I found it difficult to stay engaged with the curriculum and in the COVID-19 pandemic, I felt uncertain about my academic direction. As I had an interest in physics, I began exploring some online courses on mathematics and self-study. As a result my undergraduate results weren’t stellar, but the journey helped me to discover my passion for the intersection of biology, mathematics, and computation.

I’m currently focused on expanding my skills in systems biology, population genetics, and statistical modeling. My long-term goal is to pursue a research career that bridges computational methods with life sciences to solve complex biological questions.
